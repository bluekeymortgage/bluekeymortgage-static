# Reverse Mortgages in Ontario: A Senior's Guide
*Understanding home equity conversion mortgages for Ontario homeowners 55+*

---

## What Is a Reverse Mortgage?

A reverse mortgage (officially called a **home equity conversion mortgage** in Canada) is a loan that allows homeowners aged 55+ to convert a portion of their home equity into cash. Unlike a traditional mortgage, **no monthly principal and interest payments are required**.

### Key Features for Ontario Seniors
- **Age requirement:** 55+ (younger spouse can be 54)
- **Home ownership:** Must own your home (or have a small remaining mortgage)
- **Primary residence:** Must live in the home as your main residence
- **No monthly payments:** No required payments while you live in the home
- **Tax‑free proceeds:** Money received is not considered taxable income
- **Non‑recourse protection:** You/heirs never owe more than the home's value
- **Equity protection:** Regulations limit how much you can access (typically up to 55% of home value)
- **Stay in your home:** You keep the title and can stay as long as it's your primary residence

## How Reverse Mortgages Work in Ontario

### The Basic Process
1. **Eligibility Assessment:** Age 55+, own your home, live there as primary residence
2. **Home Appraisal:** Professional appraisal determines current market value
3. **Independent Legal Advice:** Mandatory consultation with a lawyer to ensure understanding
4. **Application & Approval:** Lender review and approval process
5. **Funding:** Choose how to receive your money (options below)
6. **Ongoing:** No monthly payments, but must maintain property and pay taxes/insurance

### How Much Can You Access?
The amount depends on:
- **Your age** (older = higher percentage)
- **Home value** (current market value)
- **Location** (Ontario property values)
- **Interest rates** (current market rates)

**Typical range:** 25‑55% of your home's current value

**Example:** A $750,000 home with homeowners aged 70 might access $300,000‑$400,000

### How You Receive Your Money
- **Lump sum:** One‑time payment
- **Monthly payments:** Regular income stream
- **Line of credit:** Draw as needed, balance grows only on what you use
- **Combination:** Mix of the above options

## Common Myths vs. Facts

### Myth 1: "The bank takes your home"
**Fact:** You keep the title and ownership. The lender has a lien (like a traditional mortgage) but you remain the homeowner. You can stay as long as it's your primary residence.

### Myth 2: "You can owe more than your home is worth"
**Fact:** Canadian reverse mortgages are **non‑recourse loans**. If the loan balance exceeds the home's value when it's sold, neither you nor your heirs are responsible for the difference. The lender's insurance covers the shortfall.

### Myth 3: "Your children won't inherit anything"
**Fact:** Your heirs inherit any remaining equity. They can choose to:
- Repay the loan and keep the home
- Sell the home and keep the difference
- Let the lender sell the home (rare)

**Example:** Home worth $800,000 with $300,000 reverse mortgage balance = $500,000 inheritance.

### Myth 4: "It's only for people in financial trouble"
**Fact:** Many financially secure seniors use reverse mortgages for:
- Preserving other investments
- Funding travel or hobbies
- Helping family members
- Home renovations
- Tax planning

### Myth 5: "The interest compounds endlessly"
**Fact:** Interest does compound, but:
- There's a built‑in equity protection buffer (you can't access 100% of equity)
- The loan only compounds on the actual balance
- Many lines of credit work the same way

## Who Is a Good Candidate?

### Ideal Candidates
- **Homeowners 55+** who want to stay in their home
- **Seniors with substantial home equity** but limited cash flow
- **People needing home modifications** for aging in place
- **Those wanting to eliminate monthly mortgage payments**
- **Seniors helping family** with education or down payments
- **People preserving other assets** (RRSPs, investments)

### When to Consider Alternatives
- **Planning to move** within 5‑7 years
- **Primary goal** is to leave maximum inheritance
- **Have other liquid assets** you prefer to use first
- **Health concerns** that may require moving to care soon

## Alternatives to Reverse Mortgages

### Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)
- **Requires monthly interest payments**
- Variable interest rates
- Can be called by the lender
- Requires income qualification

### Refinancing
- **Monthly payments required**
- New mortgage at current rates
- May extend amortization
- Requires income qualification

### Downsizing
- **Sell current home, buy smaller one**
- Free up cash from sale proceeds
- May reduce maintenance costs
- Emotional attachment to current home

### Government Programs
- **Home Adaptations for Seniors' Independence (HASI)**
- **Ontario Seniors' Home Safety Tax Credit**
- **Various municipal programs**

## The Ontario Reverse Mortgage Process

### Step 1: Education & Exploration
- Learn how reverse mortgages work
- Understand costs and benefits
- Get preliminary numbers
- No obligation or cost

### Step 2: Independent Legal Advice (Required)
- Meet with a lawyer experienced in reverse mortgages
- Review all documents and implications
- Ensure you fully understand the commitment
- This is a legal requirement in Canada

### Step 3: Application & Appraisal
- Complete application with help from your mortgage agent
- Professional home appraisal
- Credit check (minimal impact)
- Lender review

### Step 4: Closing & Funding
- Sign final documents with lawyer
- Receive funds according to your chosen method
- Begin using your home equity

### Step 5: Ongoing
- No monthly payments
- Must maintain property insurance and taxes
- Must keep home as primary residence
- Can make voluntary payments if desired

## Questions to Ask Yourself

### Before Considering a Reverse Mortgage

2. What are my other financial resources?
3. What are my goals for this money?

2. How much can I realistically access?
3. What happens if I need to move for health reasons?
4. How is the interest calculated?
5. What are my repayment options?
6. Can you provide references from other senior clients?
