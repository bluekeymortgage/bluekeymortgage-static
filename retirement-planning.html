Retirement Income Sources in Ontario

A balanced retirement income plan combines government benefits, personal savings, and home equity:

Government Pension Plans: CPP (Canada Pension Plan), OAS (Old Age Security), GIS (Guaranteed Income Supplement)
Personal Savings: RRSPs, TFSAs, pensions from former employers
Home Equity: Reverse mortgages, HELOCs, downsizing proceeds
Investment Income: Dividends, interest, capital gains
Part-Time Work: Supplement income while staying active

Ontario Retirement Tax Considerations

Understanding how retirement income is taxed in Ontario helps maximize your financial security.

Key Tax Points for Ontario Seniors

  • OAS & GIS: Taxable income (except GIS for lowest-income seniors)
  • CPP: Taxable income
  • RRSP/RRIF withdrawals: Taxable as income
  • Reverse mortgage proceeds: NOT taxable income
  • TFSA withdrawals: NOT taxable
  • Age 65+ tax credits: Age credit, pension income credit
  • Medical expense credits: Many senior healthcare costs qualify

Always consult a tax professional for personalized advice.
